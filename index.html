Our Story

Paivepo —
"There Was"

The name comes from a Shona word Tinashe Kachama's late grandmother would use to begin her stories. "Paivepo…" — the moment she said it, eyes would widen and everyone would brace for whatever tale was coming next.

Today, Paivepo has become something larger than one story. It is a home — for artists, makers and creators whose work deserves to be seen by the world. Based at Old Nick Village in Plettenberg Bay, we bring together African art, sculpture, furniture and storytelling under one roof.

Paivepo is a bridge – connecting collectors around the world with overlooked African artists whose work deserves to be seen.

The Founder

Tinashe Kachama —
a story of becoming

01

The Beginning

Tinashe Kachama arrived in South Africa carrying his grandmother's stories and an instinct for beauty. He founded Paivepo in 2021 at Old Nick Village — a creative hub in Plettenberg Bay that felt immediately like the right place for the work he was imagining.

02

The Craft

His beaded sculptures begin with an idea — inspired by African culture, nature, or memory. "Selecting the beads is an emotional part. Every colour tells something." The threading that follows is slow and meditative: bead by bead, in quiet focus, traditional African rhythms playing in the background.

03

The Collective

As Paivepo grew, Tinashe began inviting other artists into the space — painters like William Mwale and Allick, whose work shares the same commitment to craft, story and African expression. Today, Paivepo is not one artist. It is a home for many.

Artist Story

Allick – From Zimbabwe to the Garden Route

How a Zimbabwean artist found his voice in Plettenberg Bay, painting the stories of his homeland and the wild beauty of the South African coast.

Read the full story → Allick grew up in Zimbabwe, surrounded by the vibrant colours of Shona art and the rhythms of everyday life. After moving to South Africa, he settled in the Garden Route, where the landscape and light transformed his work. Today, his canvases are a conversation between memory and place – bold, expressive, and deeply African. His paintings are collected by art lovers who recognise the soul in every brushstroke.

Artist Story

William's Journey from Malawi

How one painter crossed borders to find his canvas in the Garden Route of South Africa. The landscapes that shaped him. The colours he found waiting here.

Read the full story → William Mwale left Malawi with little more than a paintbrush and a dream. He settled in Plettenberg Bay, where the dramatic skies and wild coastline reminded him of home. Today, his oils capture the soul of African wildlife – each stroke a memory of the vast plains he left behind. His work has been collected from Cape Town to London, but he remains rooted in the Garden Route, painting the Africa he carries inside him.

Published 16 June 2026

Heritage

The Story Behind Paivepo – A Grandmother's Word

A Shona word passed down through generations. The weight of memory, story, and a world that once was.

Read the full story → "Paivepo" – the word Tinashe Kachama's grandmother used to begin her tales. It means "There was..." and carried the promise of something profound about to unfold. When she spoke it, the room fell silent, eyes widened, and the story took hold.

Tinashe carries that same power into every piece at Paivepo. It's not just a name – it's an invitation. An invitation to listen, to wonder, and to become part of a story that began long before we arrived, and will continue long after we're gone. Every sculpture, every painting, every handcrafted object carries that same quiet power – the power of a story waiting to be told.

Published 16 June 2026
